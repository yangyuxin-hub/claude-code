Good Friction vs Bad Friction - Onboarding Design Principle

Layout: two-column comparison with clear visual separation

ZONES:
- Zone 1 (top): Headline "Not All Friction Is Equal" in Near Black (#1A1A1A)
- Zone 2 (left column — Bad Friction): Light Red/Coral background (#FEF2F2). Header: "Bad Friction ✗" in red. Three items with X icons:
  · "Unnecessary form fields"
  · "Steps that slow users down with no payoff"
  · "Friction that adds confusion, not clarity"
  · Bottom result: "User abandons → Lower conversion"
- Zone 3 (right column — Good Friction): Light Green background (#F0FDF4). Header: "Good Friction ✓" in Emerald (#10B981). Three items with checkmark icons:
  · "Onboarding quiz: 'What do you use this for?'"
  · "Step-by-step: helps users understand the product"
  · "Answers: 'Why is this right for me?'"
  · Bottom result: "User commits → Higher conversion + retention"
- Zone 4 (center divider): Bold vertical line with label "The Key Question" and arrow pointing right: "Does this step help the user understand why this product fits them?"
- Zone 5 (bottom): Amber callout bar: "Mercury result: splitting one page into two steps → conversion UP. Anthropic onboarding: more steps → data looks great."
